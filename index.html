Ideal Job

An ideal job would be a VST(plugins) and DAW (Virtual Studio Technology, DIGITAL AUDIO WORKSTATION) Developer.

https://www.ableton.com/en/jobs/apply/85991/





In this role I would be creating and testing software designed to emulate the equivalent of real-world recording and production studio technology. I have my own studio and equipment as well as a large library of VSTs. With years of interest and practice in musical technology, it would be a given that this area of IT and software would be of interest to me. Working in a role such as this would allow me to merge IT with another area of interest of mine(music creation, capture and manipulation) and allow the two to not only bounce off each other but to maintain an interest in more classical programming through integration with more creative thinking and technology.

The skills required in this area are not incredibly broad but do fall under two vastly different schools of thought. The first being the "IT and programming" school. This would encapsulate skills such as Excellent C/C++ coding skills. C++ and C are the go-to languages for VST development. The execution speed of this language makes it really the only language that can work with audio in real time. Experience designing and implementing procedural audio software is a must in this area. There is no room for latency when using VST technology. Understanding of Swift and Objective-C are necessary, as most VSTs are expected to work on both Apple and Windows products. I would also have to have experience with coding DAW software.

The Second "school of thought" would be the actual hardware we are attempting to replicate using software. This would be understanding of audio, acoustics, physics, and procedural audio. This would include understanding not only how the hardware works but what it is actually trying to accomplish. It's one thing to understand that a compressor reduces audio signals above a threshold but is another to understand why/how you would want to do it in an audio mix. There are no right ways of doing things in audio but plenty of wrong ways is another way of saying it. I would have to be able to understand what a plugin is trying to accomplish acoustically or signal wise and then be able to translate its purpose into code.

I would have to have a good understanding of acoustical theory and how we can alter audio files using signal processors. A bachelor in something such as BCH IT( software development) or software engineering would be highly appropriate though not required as examples of previous work is most important(seeing as you can develop working VSTs independently).

And finally, good User-centred Design is critical as a good interface for a plugin can make or break it on the market.

Currently I have the eqivalent of say a start of second year uni programmer with C++. I have a good understanding of the major and most lesser known signal processors(compressors, EQs, limiters, saturators, harmonic exciters etc etc.) and how/where to apply them. I also have good knowledge on the use of some DAW programs. I have done post production work using this type of equipment on multiple projects for other artists and understand the requirements of any producer who would be using this software.

Most of the further required skills centralise around the software side. My understanding of the audio side is already at the minimum for this position. Primarily the degree in IT with a major in software development would be a great help towards getting the experience I need to obtain this position. However this would have to be supplimented with the extra activites such as the development of independent crowd source created plugins to develop the neceassary experience required for this position. I would have to start with developing more simple plugins (such as monophonic* sythesisers and basic compressors) and work towards eventually developing more complex plugins. This would not only gain me much-needed experience but would also facilitate the creation of a folio of plugins I would have created.

to summarise this plan I would:

1. Finish my degree at RMIT, while
2. develop simpler plugins, then
3. develop more complex plugins
4. expand and publish my folio for use and advertisement
5. apply for positions once I have made professional grade plugins.


*Polyphony is a property of musical instruments that means that they can play multiple independent melody lines simultaneously. Instruments featuring polyphony are said to be polyphonic. Instruments that are not capable of polyphony are monophonic or paraphonic.-https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyphony_and_monophony_in_instruments
